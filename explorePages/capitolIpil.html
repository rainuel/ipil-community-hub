Zamboanga Sibugay Capitol

Ipil is the provincial capital of Zamboanga Sibugay, located in the island of Mindanao, Philippines. The capitol complex sits on a hill in Ipil Heights and is noted for its colorful, palace-like architecture that blends modern design with local culturally inspired elements.

History

Ipil’s provincial capitol traces its roots to the province’s formation in 2001, symbolizing unity and identity through its hilltop location and culturally inspired architecture.

Events

The capitol hosts cultural festivals such as the Sibug-Sibug Festival, along with government functions, civic gatherings, and commemorations.

Nearby Attractions

Nearby attractions include the Rotunda Obelisk, hilltop viewpoints, beaches, mangrove parks, waterfalls, and cultural sites in nearby towns.
