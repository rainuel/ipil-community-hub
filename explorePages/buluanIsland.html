Buluan Island

Buluan Island is a small, tranquil island off Ipil in Zamboanga Sibugay, known for its white-sand beaches, clear turquoise waters, and relatively undeveloped, secluded shorelines.

History

Buluan Island’s brief history: it emerged as a developing coastal destination after Zamboanga Sibugay’s formation in 2001, built up as a protected, low-traffic beach site known for its white sands and tranquil shores.

Events

Buluan Island's short event history: it was designated as a protected marine sanctuary in 2014, with ongoing efforts for conservation and eco-tourism development.

Nearby Attractions

Nearby attractions around Buluan Island include other Zamboanga Sibugay beach spots and neighboring nature sights such as Sta. Cruz Island in nearby Zamboanga City for day-trips, mangrove areas, and falls like Tagbilat/Malagandis Falls, all popular for snorkeling, boat trips, and coastal views.
