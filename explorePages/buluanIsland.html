Buluan Island

Buluan Island is a small, tranquil island off Ipil in Zamboanga Sibugay, known for its white-sand beaches, clear turquoise waters, and relatively undeveloped, secluded shorelines.

History

Buluan Island emerged as a developing coastal destination after Zamboanga Sibugay’s formation in 2001, known for its protected, low-traffic white-sand beaches.

Events

Designated as a protected marine sanctuary in 2014, with ongoing conservation and eco-tourism efforts.

Nearby Attractions

Nearby attractions include other Sibugay beaches, mangrove areas, and waterfalls such as Tagbilat and Malagandis Falls.
